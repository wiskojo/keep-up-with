# Reference Update: Fable/Mythos DC Meetings

Cheyenne Haslett, a POLITICO White House reporter covering health and tech, and Hadas Gold, CNN's AI correspondent in New York, both posted new process details from the Fable/Mythos talks in DC.

Haslett says Monday was the first day of in-person meetings after Anthropic sent senior safety and policy people to Washington. The meetings were staff-led and technical, with Commerce and ONCD involved. ONCD is the White House Office of the National Cyber Director. CAISI is Commerce's AI safety/standards institute, and Haslett says Chris Fall, its director, was part of the staff-level process.

The timing still sounds unresolved. Haslett reports that a senior White House official said it will likely take more than a few days, though it could move quickly.

> It will likely take longer than a few days to reach a resolution, a senior White House official said, but left the door open to the possibility that it can be done quickly. "That's up to Anthropic," the official said.
>
> _Cheyenne Haslett, POLITICO White House reporter, 23 likes / 3.4k impressions when checked_

Anthropic's line to Gold is softer: senior technical staff met with officials after earlier virtual meetings, and both sides are working quickly toward protecting US critical infrastructure and the US lead in cyber defense.
